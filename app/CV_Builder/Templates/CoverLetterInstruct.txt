1. Header: Include your contact information (name, phone number, email, LinkedIn profile)
and the date. If you have the details, also include the hiring manager's name and
company address.
2. Salutation: Address the hiring manager directly (e.g., "Dear [Hiring Manager's Name]"). If
you don’t know the name, “Dear Hiring Manager” is a suitable alternative.
3. Introduction: Start with a strong opening sentence that grabs attention. Briefly introduce
yourself and state the position you are applying for. If you have a mutual contact or a
unique reason for your interest in the company, mention it here.
4. Body (1-2 paragraphs):
• First Paragraph: Explain why you are interested in this role and the company.
Show that you've done your research and understand what the company does.
• Second Paragraph: Highlight relevant experiences, skills, and achievements. Use
specific examples to demonstrate how your background makes you a great fit for
the job. If you have metrics or statistics to back up your achievements, include
them.
5. Closing Paragraph: Reiterate your enthusiasm for the role. Mention how you could
contribute to the company's goals. State that you’re looking forward to the opportunity
to discuss your application further.
6. Formal Closing: End with a professional closing like “Sincerely” or “Best regards,”
followed by your full name.
7. Postscript (Optional): A postscript (P.S.) can be an effective way to highlight an
important detail or add a personal touch.
